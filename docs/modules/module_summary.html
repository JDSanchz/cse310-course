CS 246 | Software Design and Development

Module Summary

Overview

In this course, you will select four discovery modules and two language modules to complete individually. You will also be asked to complete a larger project as a team. The team project can focus on one or more of these modules.

When you complete your Course Goals Survey as part of 01-Prove, you will identify your current knowledge and your learning goals. Based on your knowledge and goals, you will select the best modules to complete during this course. You are allowed to change the modules you select throughout the semester. It is important that you select modules for which you have an interest but little or no experience.

Each of the modules contains an overview, requirements, stretch challenges, guidance, and a grading rubric. The modules do not contain detailed instructions. In most cases, you will be given latitude to select your own programming languages, tools, and software functionality. However, you are strongly encouraged to use the Guidance section in each module to get started. In every module, you will be required to put your code and supporting documentation into your personal software portfolio hosted in GitHub.

Prior to starting a module, you will submit a module plan document. You should not delay writing and submitting your plan. When you complete a module, you will re-submit the plan document to include the results. The results section of the document will include the link(s) to your GitHub repository and other summarizing information. The Prove assignments in the course will provide the templates for these documents, including related instructions.

When selecting a discovery module, you should consider the pre-requisite knowledge shown below.
