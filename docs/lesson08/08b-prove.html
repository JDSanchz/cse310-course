Finding a Job: Resumes

The Self Reliance Services (SRS) “Find a Better Job” manual is intended for use in a group, but you will use it individually. Activities and discussions in the reading should be done individually.

This week, you should complete the following reading:

  • Chapter 5, Introduction, Pages 77–78

  • Chapter 5, Section 1—Three Simple Principles, Page 79

  • Chapter 5, Section 2—Resumes, Pages 79–85

    • In the first activity, you will first need to find an entry-level or internship job that you are interested in. You can search on websites like GlassDoor, Indeed, or BYU-Idaho Handshake. You can find more resources at the BYU-Idaho Career Center.

    • In the second activity, share your accomplishments with two or more friends or family members and ask for feedback.

  • Chapter 5, Conclusion, Page 89

    • You should watch the video Better Written Materials referenced in this section.

Additionally, you should spend a few minutes looking at the resume resources at the BYU-Idaho Career Center.

Assignment

During the reading from the SRS “Finding a Better Job” manual, you completed activities to begin the process of writing a resume. Your assignment this week is to write a resume following the guidance and examples in the reading. There are additional examples available at the BYU-Idaho Career Center. Frequently, job seekers who are still in college without much employment experience will put their education first on the resume.

  1. Download the cse310_resume.docx template.

  2. Write your resume in a separate file.

  3. Copy the resume to the template for submission.
